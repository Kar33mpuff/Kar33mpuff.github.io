Harriton High School CTE

Classes

Foundations of Engineering

Students learn the steps of the technological process. Using these steps, the students design and build projects that are assigned. This class starts at the beginning, drafting, and works its way down the engineering process, all the way to production methods, like 3D printing and CNC.

Innovation and Invention Robotics

Students learn to program and build robots that compete in interscholastic events. These events, normally designed by the tech ed teacher, will create a fun learning environment for aspiring technology students. This class puts power in the hands of the students, to build a robot to solve an open ended chalenge

Comp-Sci

Students learn the basics of coding and computer design. Often projects will include basic website creation, or coding for a number output as practice. Students in the lower compsci classes will start with drag and drop programming, wheras the AP compsci courses immediately delve into JavaScript and Python.

Teachers

Travis Lehman

Mr. Lehman is the Tech Ed teacher at Harriton High School, as well as the VEX Robotics coach and the teacher in charge of Harriton High School TV. He was formerly Harriton's TSA advisor. At Harriton, he teaches not only the typical Tech Ed courses, but other unique ones, like video production.

Chris Busza

Mr. Busza teaches math and computer science. He teaches computer science at the honors level and AP level. In his computer science classes, students get exposure to languages like JavaScript.
